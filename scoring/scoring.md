 Let's reiterate how our calculation works for the more complicated marathon category (team-based):

1. Ascents are awarded base points based on the grade of the route ascented. Base points are set on the scored_ascents table.
2. Volume bonus is awarded on a TEAM basis. 25 points are awarded every 5 ascents. i.e. 10 ascents award 50 points to the team, while 9 ascents award only 25 volume bonus points. This does not apply on the scored ascents table as it is determined on an overall team basis, so it is only on the final rankings table.
3. Team ascent bonus is awarded only on ascents that all team members have completed and a different bonus factor is applied depending on team size. i.e. if in a team of 3, all three participants complete the same route, then a bonus factor of 18% is added to each of those ascents that qualify. These should apply to the scored ascents table for each ascent.
4. A unique ascent bonus factor is added to an ascent, when that ascent is on a route that only one person has climbed accross the whole marathon category (unique). An ascent from a solo participant (who automatically only participates in the boulder beasts category, does not get included in the assessment of uniqueness.)
5. Master grade bonus is applied to the team that climbed the most routes at a given grade. This is done on a team-basis, therefore it is not on the scored ascents table, but on the final rankings table.
6. All team scores are divided by the team size for normalization and fairness. Final ranking tables must be shown with normalized scores, including bonuses, so that they can be compared like for like. We could have a more detailed staging table with all gross score components and bonuses before normalization for granular data inspection.
